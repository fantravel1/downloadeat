Tourist Reality Check

✅

Croatia is a top tourist destination and food delivery works well for visitors. Wolt dominates the market and is the easiest option for tourists. Zagreb has full delivery coverage with hundreds of restaurants. Split and Dubrovnik have growing coverage that expands significantly during the summer tourist season.

💡

Coastal tourist cities are well-served in summer. Croatian cuisine is Mediterranean-influenced with amazing seafood. Ordering seafood delivery on the coast is an excellent experience. Many restaurants that serve tourists in-person also partner with Wolt and Glovo for delivery. Cash on delivery also works if your card has issues.

⚠️

Smaller islands and rural areas have limited coverage. Delivery apps work best in Zagreb, Split, Dubrovnik, Rijeka, and Zadar. If you're staying in a small coastal village or on one of the smaller islands, you may not have delivery access. Download Wolt before you arrive and check coverage for your specific location.

Tipping Delivery Drivers in Croatia

Tipping delivery drivers in Croatia is common but not required. Small tips are appreciated and are the cultural norm.

  • Standard tip: 10-20 HRK (1-3 EUR) is a typical tip for delivery drivers
  • Not mandatory: Unlike the US, tips are not expected or required in Croatia
  • In-app tipping: Wolt supports in-app tipping, making it easy to tip without cash
  • Round up: Many people simply round up to the nearest convenient amount

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Getting food delivered to your accommodation in Croatia is straightforward. Here are some tips to make it smooth.

🏨

Hotels and Airbnbs both work well for delivery. Most hotels in Zagreb have no issue with food delivery ‚Äî just meet the driver in the lobby. For coastal apartments and Airbnbs, share detailed location details and any gate codes. Drivers in tourist areas are accustomed to delivering to vacation rentals. Add a landmark or the apartment name in the delivery notes for best results.

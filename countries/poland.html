Grocery Delivery in Poland

Poland has several grocery delivery options, from rapid delivery services to full supermarket orders. Prices are very competitive by European standards.

  • Glovo Groceries — Order from local supermarkets through Glovo. Quick delivery using the same app you use for food. Accepts foreign cards.
  • Lisek — Rapid grocery delivery (15-20 minutes) in Warsaw and other major cities. Small but well-curated selection of essentials and snacks.
  • Biedronka Online — Poland's most popular discount supermarket with online ordering. Wide selection and very competitive prices.
  • Frisco — Online supermarket with a large selection. Scheduled delivery windows. Good for stocking up on supplies for a longer stay.

Tourist Reality Check

What You Actually Need to Know

Poland has one of the most competitive food delivery markets in Europe with five major apps fighting for customers. This is great for tourists because it means lots of options, frequent promotions, and competitive prices.

Warsaw and Krakow have outstanding delivery coverage. Both cities are popular tourist destinations with a wide range of restaurants available on delivery apps. Polish food is incredibly affordable by Western European standards. A full meal delivery might cost 25-45 PLN (roughly 6-10 EUR), including delivery fees.

All major apps accept foreign credit cards. Poland uses the Polish zloty (PLN), not the Euro. Your card handles currency conversion automatically. Cash on delivery is also available on most platforms, which is useful as a backup.

English support is improving across all apps. Uber Eats and Wolt have the best English interfaces. Restaurant menus may be in Polish, but most include photos that make ordering straightforward. Younger Poles (especially in cities) generally speak good English.

Building intercoms may require an apartment number to buzz in. If you are staying in a Polish apartment building, make sure you have the intercom code. Drivers will usually call or message you through the app if they cannot find the entrance.

Tipping for Delivery in Poland

Tipping Culture

Small tips are appreciated in Poland and becoming more common for delivery. A tip of 5-10 PLN (roughly 1-2 EUR) is considered generous. There is no strong expectation to tip, but Poles increasingly tip delivery drivers, especially in larger cities.

Most apps offer in-app tipping options. You can also hand cash directly to the driver. Tipping is not as ingrained as in the United States, but it is a nice gesture that drivers appreciate.

Hotel & Airbnb Delivery

Getting Food to Your Door

Hotels in Poland generally allow food delivery. Use the hotel's full address and plan to meet the driver in the lobby for the smoothest handoff.

For apartments, share the intercom code with the driver through the in-app chat. Polish apartment buildings typically have intercom systems. Make sure you know your apartment number and the name on the buzzer. Adding clear delivery notes with your flat number and floor helps drivers find you quickly.

In Krakow's Old Town, some streets are pedestrian-only. Drivers will park at the nearest accessible point and walk to your location. Be ready to meet them or provide clear walking directions in the delivery notes.

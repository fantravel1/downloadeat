Tourist Reality Check

The UK is the EASIEST European Country for Delivery

For English-speaking tourists, the UK is the absolute easiest country in Europe for food delivery. Everything is natively in English — no translations needed, no language barriers at all. Every major app accepts foreign credit cards without any issues. No local phone number is required. You can literally land at Heathrow, open Deliveroo or Uber Eats, and order food to your hotel within minutes. It does not get easier than this.

London Has Incredible Coverage

London is one of the best cities in the world for food delivery. The diversity of cuisine is staggering — Indian, Chinese, Thai, Japanese, Middle Eastern, Italian, British, African, Caribbean, and much more. Coverage extends across all London zones, from Central London to the outer boroughs. Other major UK cities like Manchester, Birmingham, Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Bristol also have excellent delivery coverage. Even smaller towns are covered by Just Eat.

Fish & Chips Delivery Is a Must-Try

You cannot visit the UK without trying fish and chips, and delivery is one of the best ways to enjoy it. Just Eat has the widest selection of traditional fish and chip shops (chippies). Look for well-rated local shops rather than chains for the most authentic experience. Other British delivery classics include a full English breakfast (available for brunch delivery), curry (the UK is famous for its Indian restaurants), and Sunday roast.

Tipping & Etiquette

Tipping on food delivery in the UK is optional but appreciated. A tip of £1–2 is common and considered generous. Delivery apps show a tipping option after your order is delivered. Many UK customers do tip on delivery orders, but it is not required or expected. Drivers will not be offended if you do not tip.

UK delivery drivers are professional and courteous. Delivery instructions are straightforward since everything is in English. If you have a complex address, add a note. "Leave at door" instructions are common and well-understood in the UK. Contactless delivery became standard during the pandemic and remains popular.

Hotel Delivery

Hotels in the UK are very delivery-friendly. Use the full hotel address and add the hotel name in your delivery notes. Most hotel receptionists will accept delivery orders without question. Many London hotels have a specific area for delivery pickups.

For flats and apartments, share the buzzer code with the driver in your delivery notes. UK apartment buildings typically have intercom systems. Student halls and some managed buildings may have restrictions on delivery access — check with your host. For Airbnb flats, the host usually provides entry instructions that work well for delivery drivers too.

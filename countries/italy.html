Tourist Reality Check

Good Delivery Scene in Major Cities

Italy's delivery scene is solid in major cities. Rome, Milan, Florence, and Naples all have excellent coverage through multiple apps. Italian food culture is very restaurant-focused, so the quality of delivered food is often outstanding. All major apps accept foreign credit cards, and English support is available on the main platforms. Pizza delivery is obviously legendary in Italy.

Italian Restaurant Hours Matter

Italian restaurants follow strict schedules: lunch is 12:30–3:00 PM and dinner runs 7:30–11:00 PM. Many restaurants close between 3:00–7:30 PM. During the afternoon gap, delivery options are more limited — you will mostly find pizzerias, fast-food chains, and bakeries. Plan your delivery orders around these hours for the best selection of authentic Italian cuisine.

The Citofono (Intercom) Challenge

Italian apartment buildings use intercoms called "citofono." Delivery to apartments requires sharing the intercom name (usually the surname on the buzzer) with the driver. For Airbnb stays, ask your host for the citofono name. In delivery notes, write: "Citofono: [Name], [Floor] piano" (piano means floor). If the driver cannot reach you, they will call. Having "Scendo" (I'm coming down) ready helps with communication.

Tipping & Etiquette

Tipping for food delivery is not traditional in Italy. A small tip of 1 EUR is appreciated but definitely not expected. Italian delivery culture is straightforward — the price you see is the price you pay. Delivery apps include a tip option after delivery, but most Italian customers do not use it. As a tourist, a small tip is a nice gesture, particularly in bad weather or for late-night orders.

Italian drivers are generally professional and efficient. If you have a complicated delivery location (no elevator, high floor, confusing building), a tip is especially appreciated. Simply saying "Grazie" (thank you) with a smile goes a long way in Italy.

Hotel Delivery

Hotels in Italy are generally accommodating with food delivery. Use the hotel's full address and add "Hotel [Name]" in your delivery notes. Most hotel receptionists will accept delivery orders on your behalf. Italian hotels in tourist areas are very familiar with delivery services.

For B&Bs and apartments, share the intercom name (citofono) with the driver in the delivery instructions. Include the floor number using the Italian convention: "primo piano" (first floor), "secondo piano" (second floor), etc. For buildings without an elevator (many older Italian buildings), mention this so the driver knows what to expect. Meeting the driver at the main entrance is always a reliable option.

Top Food Delivery Apps in South Africa

South Africa has a mature and well-developed food delivery market. English is an official language, so there are zero language barriers. All major apps accept foreign credit cards. Cape Town, Johannesburg, Durban, and Pretoria have excellent coverage. The food scene is diverse — from braai (BBQ) and biltong to Cape Malay cuisine, Indian food in Durban, and world-class restaurants.

Uber Eats

Food Delivery — Dominant Platform
Top Pick

Uber Eats is the dominant food delivery platform in South Africa with the widest restaurant selection and best coverage across the country. It works exactly as you expect — your existing Uber account transfers seamlessly, your foreign credit card works immediately, and the interface is entirely in English. Coverage spans Cape Town, Johannesburg, Pretoria, Durban, Port Elizabeth, Stellenbosch, and many smaller cities. Restaurant selection is excellent, from local favorites to international chains.

Mr D Food

Food Delivery — Local Competitor
Local Favorite

Mr D Food is South Africa's homegrown food delivery platform, owned by Takealot (South Africa's Amazon equivalent). It has a strong restaurant selection with many exclusive partnerships with popular South African chains and restaurants not found on Uber Eats. The app is entirely in English, accepts foreign credit cards, and has competitive delivery fees. Mr D Food is particularly strong in suburban areas and has a loyal local following. Worth downloading alongside Uber Eats for the widest restaurant access.

Bolt Food

Food Delivery — Growing Platform

Bolt Food is part of the Bolt super app (previously known as Taxify), which many tourists already use for ride-hailing in South Africa. The food delivery section is growing and offers competitive pricing with lower delivery fees than competitors. Restaurant selection is more limited than Uber Eats or Mr D Food, but expanding. If you already have Bolt installed for rides, the food delivery section is convenient and accepts the same payment methods.

Grocery & Convenience Delivery

South Africa has outstanding grocery delivery options that rival or exceed many developed countries. These are game-changers for tourists.

Checkers Sixty60

Grocery Delivery — 60-Minute Promise
Game-Changer

Checkers Sixty60 is a revolutionary grocery delivery service that promises delivery from Checkers supermarkets in 60 minutes. It has transformed grocery shopping in South Africa. The selection is extensive — fresh produce, meat, dairy, household items, snacks, drinks, and even wine. The app is English-only, accepts card payments, and covers most urban and suburban areas. If you are staying in an Airbnb or self-catering accommodation, this is indispensable.

Pick n Pay Asap!

Grocery Delivery — Quick Service

Pick n Pay Asap! is Pick n Pay's quick grocery delivery service, competing directly with Checkers Sixty60. It offers delivery from Pick n Pay supermarkets with a similar speed promise. Great selection of groceries and household items. The app is straightforward and English-only. Another excellent option for stocking up your accommodation.

Woolworths Dash

Grocery Delivery — Premium Option

Woolworths Dash is the delivery service from Woolworths, South Africa's premium grocery chain (similar to Marks & Spencer in the UK). Woolworths is known for high-quality fresh food, ready meals, and specialty items. Dash delivers from Woolworths stores with competitive timing. Perfect for premium groceries, ready-to-eat meals, and quality products. Accepts card payments.

Tourist Reality Check

South Africa is a great country for food delivery as a tourist. Here are the practical details.

English Makes Everything Easy

English is one of South Africa's 11 official languages and is the primary language of business and commerce. Every delivery app, restaurant menu, and communication with drivers will be in English. There are zero language barriers. This makes South Africa one of the easiest countries in Africa for food delivery as a tourist.

Gated Communities Are Standard

Many residential areas in South Africa, especially in Johannesburg and Pretoria, are in gated communities or security estates. Delivery drivers are very familiar with this and know the protocols. You may need to provide a gate code, call security to let the driver in, or meet them at the gate. Hotels and guesthouses in these areas handle this routinely. It is not a hindrance — just part of the local delivery culture.

Card Preferred Over Cash

Unlike many African countries, South Africa has a strong card payment culture. Cash on delivery is available but card payment is preferred and recommended. Using your foreign credit card through the app is the most convenient and secure payment method. South Africa's payment infrastructure is modern and reliable.

Load Shedding May Affect Delivery

South Africa sometimes experiences load shedding (scheduled power outages). While delivery apps continue to function on mobile data, some restaurants may close or have limited menus during power outages. Delivery times may be affected. Check the load shedding schedule (apps like EskomSePush track this) and order during periods when power is on for the best experience.

Tipping & Delivery Etiquette

Tipping is expected in South Africa and is an important part of service culture.

Tips Are Expected (10-15%)

South African delivery workers depend on tips as part of their income. A tip of 10-15% or a minimum of R10-R20 is expected. You can tip through the app or give cash directly to the driver. For good service, tip generously — delivery workers in South Africa often face challenging conditions. Tipping is a deeply embedded part of South African service culture.

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Delivery to your accommodation works well across South Africa.

Hotels and Guesthouses Work Well

Hotels, guesthouses, and B&Bs across South Africa accommodate food delivery. Cape Town's waterfront hotels, Johannesburg's Sandton hotels, and Durban's beachfront properties all work smoothly with delivery apps. Drivers come to the lobby or reception area. Guesthouses in suburbs are also well-served.

Safari Lodges: Probably Not

Safari lodges and game reserves are typically in remote areas without delivery coverage. If you are on safari in Kruger, Pilanesberg, or other reserves, do not expect food delivery. These lodges provide meals as part of the experience. Food delivery is for city and suburban stays only.

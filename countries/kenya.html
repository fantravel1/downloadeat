Top Food Delivery Apps in Kenya

Kenya's food delivery market is concentrated in Nairobi, with Mombasa having some coverage. Glovo and Uber Eats are the main players, both working well for tourists. English is an official language, so there are no language barriers. The food scene in Nairobi is surprisingly diverse — great Indian food, Chinese cuisine, local Kenyan dishes, Ethiopian food, and international restaurants. M-Pesa (mobile money) is the dominant payment method locally, but foreign cards work on major apps.

Glovo

Food & Everything Delivery — Dominant Platform
Top Pick

Glovo is the leading delivery platform in Kenya with the widest restaurant selection in Nairobi. Beyond food, it delivers groceries, pharmacy items, and packages. The app accepts foreign credit cards and operates in English. Restaurant selection is extensive, covering everything from local Kenyan nyama choma (grilled meat) joints to Indian restaurants along Ngong Road, Chinese food, Ethiopian cuisine, and international chains. Coverage is strongest in Nairobi's Westlands, Karen, Kilimani, Lavington, and CBD areas. Mombasa has growing coverage.

Food Delivery — Growing Platform

Coverage focuses on Nairobi's affluent neighborhoods — Westlands, Karen, Kilimani, Gigiri, and surrounding areas. The interface is clean, English is default, and delivery tracking works well.

Grocery & Convenience Delivery

Nairobi has growing grocery delivery options that can be useful for tourists in self-catering accommodation.

Glovo Grocery

Grocery Delivery — Via Glovo App

Glovo offers grocery delivery from local supermarkets and shops within the same app. You can order water, snacks, groceries, and essentials for delivery in Nairobi. This is the most convenient option since you will already have Glovo installed for food delivery.

Carrefour Kenya Online & Naivas

Grocery Delivery — Supermarket Chains

Carrefour Kenya (operated by Majid Al Futtaim) offers online grocery ordering and delivery in Nairobi. Naivas, one of Kenya's largest supermarket chains, also has online ordering. Both have wide product selections including international items, fresh produce, and household goods. Delivery times vary but same-day delivery is common for orders placed early.

Tourist Reality Check

Here is what you need to know about food delivery in Kenya as a tourist.

Nairobi Is the Hub

Food delivery in Kenya is concentrated in Nairobi. The city has a vibrant food scene with diverse cuisine — great Indian food (large Indian community), Chinese restaurants, Ethiopian cuisine, local Kenyan dishes (nyama choma, ugali, sukuma wiki), and international options. Westlands and Karen areas are particularly well-served. Mombasa has some delivery coverage but it is much more limited.

M-Pesa Is Everywhere — But Not Required

M-Pesa is Kenya's revolutionary mobile money system and the dominant payment method for most transactions. While delivery apps support M-Pesa, tourists using foreign credit cards do not need it. Your Visa or Mastercard will work on Glovo and Uber Eats. If you get a Kenyan SIM card (available at the airport from Safaricom), you can set up M-Pesa for an even more local experience.

Safari Lodges: No Delivery

If you are going on safari in Masai Mara, Amboseli, Tsavo, or any other national park or reserve, there is no food delivery. Safari lodges and camps provide all meals as part of your stay. Food delivery is strictly for city stays in Nairobi and, to a lesser extent, Mombasa. Plan accordingly — use delivery apps during your Nairobi days.

Gated Compounds Are Standard

Many residential areas and hotels in Nairobi are in gated or secured compounds with security guards at the entrance. Delivery drivers are familiar with this and know the protocol — they check in with security, provide your name, and deliver to your door or a designated pickup point. Provide clear instructions including the compound name and any access details.

Tipping & Delivery Etiquette

Tipping is appreciated in Kenya and goes a long way for delivery workers.

Tips Appreciated (100-200 KES)

Tipping delivery drivers 100-200 KES (approximately $0.75-1.50 USD) is appreciated in Kenya. Tips are not always expected but are valued, especially for longer deliveries or deliveries during bad weather. You can tip through the app or give cash. In Kenya's cost of living context, even a small tip by tourist standards makes a meaningful difference.

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Hotel delivery works well in Nairobi's main areas.

Nairobi Hotels Allow Delivery

Hotels in Nairobi's Westlands, Karen, Kilimani, and CBD areas allow food delivery. International hotel chains (Hilton, Radisson, Fairmont) and local boutique hotels are all accustomed to delivery drivers. The driver will come to the lobby or reception. Serviced apartments and Airbnbs in residential compounds work well too.

Safari Lodges: No

Safari lodges in Masai Mara, Amboseli, and other reserves do not have delivery access. These are remote locations where all meals are provided by the lodge. Do not plan on using delivery apps during your safari stay. Use them during your Nairobi time before or after your safari.

Top Food Delivery Apps in Egypt

Egypt's food delivery scene is centered on Cairo and Alexandria, with some coverage in tourist destinations like Luxor and Sharm El Sheikh. The market is dominated by Talabat, with Uber Eats as a useful alternative in Cairo. Egyptian food delivery is remarkably affordable — expect to pay a fraction of what you would in Europe or North America. Cash on delivery is king here.

Talabat

Food Delivery — Dominant Platform
Top Pick

Talabat is the dominant food delivery platform in Egypt, just as it is across the Middle East. It has the widest restaurant selection covering everything from local Egyptian street food (koshari, ful medames, shawarma) to international chains and upscale restaurants. Talabat has English language support and accepts foreign credit cards, though cash on delivery is the most common payment method in Egypt. Coverage extends across Cairo, Alexandria, and growing in other Egyptian cities.

Payment Foreign credit cards accepted + Cash on delivery
Coverage Cairo, Alexandria, Giza, expanding to other cities
Language English support available
Tourist-Friendly Good — best option for tourists in Egypt

Uber Eats

Food Delivery — Cairo Focus

Uber Eats operates in Cairo and provides a familiar interface for tourists who use Uber at home. It accepts foreign credit cards seamlessly, has full English support, and leverages Uber's existing driver network. The restaurant selection is decent but more limited than Talabat. If you already have an Uber account, your saved payment methods will work in Egypt. Coverage is mainly in Cairo's central areas — Zamalek, Downtown, Maadi, New Cairo, and 6th of October City.

Payment Foreign credit cards accepted (Visa, Mastercard)
Coverage Cairo primarily — central areas well-covered
Language Full English support
Tourist-Friendly Good — familiar Uber interface
Payability Score 75 / 100

elmenus

Food Delivery — Local Platform
Local Favorite

elmenus is a popular Egyptian food delivery app that started as a restaurant discovery platform and expanded into delivery. It has a strong selection of local Egyptian restaurants and is well-loved by Egyptians. However, the app has limited English support and is primarily designed for Arabic speakers. Foreign card acceptance is inconsistent. It can be useful if you read Arabic or have a local friend to help, but Talabat is a more practical choice for most tourists.

Payment Limited foreign card support — cash on delivery available
Coverage Cairo, Alexandria — strong local selection
Language Limited English — primarily Arabic
Tourist-Friendly Difficult for non-Arabic speakers

Breadfast

Breakfast & Grocery Delivery

Breadfast started as a breakfast delivery service and has grown into a popular grocery and essentials delivery platform in Cairo. It is great for stocking up your hotel room with breakfast items, snacks, bread, dairy, and basic groceries. The app has a clean interface but is primarily Arabic-focused. Payment is mostly through local methods and cash. Useful for long-stay tourists but not essential for short visits.

Grocery & Convenience Delivery

Egypt has a growing grocery delivery market. These options can help you stock up your hotel room or get essentials delivered quickly.

Talabat Mart

Quick Commerce — Grocery & Essentials

Talabat Mart is Talabat's quick-commerce grocery delivery service available within the main Talabat app. It delivers groceries, snacks, drinks, and household items in as little as 20-30 minutes. Since you will already have Talabat installed for food delivery, this is the easiest grocery option for tourists. Accepts the same payment methods as Talabat food delivery.

Rabbit

Quick Commerce — Fast Grocery Delivery

Rabbit is a quick-commerce platform delivering groceries and essentials in 20 minutes in Cairo. It has grown rapidly and offers competitive prices on everyday items. The app is relatively straightforward to use, and cash on delivery is available. Great for getting water, snacks, sunscreen, and other tourist essentials delivered quickly.

InstaShop

Grocery Delivery — Supermarket Access

InstaShop connects you with local supermarkets and pharmacies in Cairo for delivery. It provides a wider selection than quick-commerce apps and lets you shop from multiple stores. Good for stocking up an Airbnb with a full grocery haul. Available in English and accepts some foreign payment methods.

Tourist Reality Check

Here is what nobody tells you about ordering food delivery in Egypt as a tourist. Read these tips before your first order.

Cash Is King for Delivery

Cash on delivery is VERY common in Egypt and is often the preferred payment method. Even when foreign cards work on Talabat, many tourists find it easier to just pay cash. Keep small bills in Egyptian pounds handy — drivers may not carry a lot of change. Exchange money at the airport or your hotel. A full meal delivery might cost 100-200 EGP ($3-6 USD), so you do not need large bills.

Drivers May Call for Directions

Egyptian delivery drivers often call to confirm your location or ask for directions. This will typically be in Arabic. Having Google Translate ready, or asking your hotel reception to speak to the driver, can resolve any issues. Alternatively, use the in-app chat with simple English messages. An Egyptian SIM card with a local number makes this easier.

Incredibly Affordable

Egyptian food delivery is incredibly affordable for tourists from Western countries. A full koshari (Egypt's national dish) might cost 30-50 EGP ($1-2 USD). A shawarma meal is often under 100 EGP. Delivery fees are minimal. You can eat very well for under $5 a meal including delivery. This makes trying different restaurants risk-free.

Cairo Traffic Means Variable Times

Cairo is one of the most congested cities in the world. Delivery times can vary wildly depending on traffic. During rush hour (roughly 8-10 AM and 4-8 PM), deliveries may take significantly longer than the app estimates. Off-peak orders are much faster. Motorcycles are the primary delivery vehicle, which helps navigate traffic, but expect delays during peak hours.

Tipping & Delivery Etiquette

Tipping is expected in Egypt and delivery drivers rely on tips as part of their income.

Tips Are Expected

Tipping delivery drivers 10-20 EGP ($0.30-0.60 USD) is expected and appreciated. Egyptian delivery workers often rely on tips to supplement their income. Always have small bills ready. If paying cash on delivery, round up generously. For larger orders or difficult deliveries (long distances, heavy items, bad weather), tip more. A 20 EGP tip goes a long way and is greatly valued.

The Bawab Can Help

If you are staying in an Egyptian apartment building, the bawab (doorman/building caretaker) can receive deliveries on your behalf. They are present 24/7 in most buildings and are accustomed to handling deliveries. Tipping your bawab 10-20 EGP for helping with deliveries is customary and builds goodwill.

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Getting food delivered to your accommodation in Egypt works well in major tourist areas.

Hotels in Tourist Areas Allow Delivery

Hotels in Cairo's tourist areas (Downtown, Zamalek, near the Pyramids, Maadi) are accustomed to food delivery. Tourist hotels in Luxor, Aswan, and Hurghada also generally allow delivery, though restaurant options may be more limited in these cities. Simply provide your hotel name and room number in the delivery instructions.

Resorts May Be Challenging

All-inclusive resorts in Sharm El Sheikh and Hurghada may not accommodate outside food delivery, especially if meals are included in your package. Check with your resort's reception. City hotels and independent guesthouses are generally flexible about food delivery.

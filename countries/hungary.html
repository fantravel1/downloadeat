Grocery Delivery in Hungary

Budapest has a growing grocery delivery scene. Options range from rapid delivery services to full supermarket orders, all at very competitive prices.

  • Wolt Market — Quick grocery delivery through the Wolt app. Essentials, snacks, drinks, and basic groceries. Uses the same international card payment as Wolt food delivery.
  • Kifli.hu — Rapid grocery delivery service in Budapest with delivery often within an hour. Good selection of fresh produce, dairy, meats, and household essentials.
  • Tesco Online — Tesco supermarkets in Hungary offer online ordering with home delivery. Wide selection and scheduled delivery windows.
  • Auchan — Major hypermarket with online ordering and home delivery. Good for large shops and stocking up.

Tourist Reality Check

What You Actually Need to Know

Budapest has a fantastic food delivery scene and Wolt makes it incredibly easy for tourists. The app works perfectly with full English support, international credit card acceptance, and a huge restaurant selection covering both Hungarian cuisine and international food.

Hungarian cuisine is rich, flavorful, and very delivery-friendly. You can order goulash (the real thing, not the tourist version), langos (deep-fried flatbread with sour cream and cheese), chicken paprikash with nokedli (Hungarian dumplings), and even chimney cake for dessert. All at prices that will make Western European tourists very happy.

Hungary uses the Hungarian forint (HUF), not the Euro. Do not be alarmed by the large numbers on menus. A meal delivery might cost 2,500-5,000 HUF (roughly 6-13 EUR). Your credit card handles currency conversion automatically.

Cash is still popular in Hungary, and some Hungarians prefer cash on delivery. But apps accept and prefer card payments, which works to your advantage as a tourist.

The ruin bar district (Jewish Quarter / District VII) is one of Budapest's most popular tourist areas, but the maze-like streets and courtyard-based bars can make delivery addresses tricky. Using a map pin drop in the app rather than typing an address will help drivers find you more easily.

Foodpanda and Bolt Food provide additional options. Having two or three apps installed gives you the widest restaurant selection and lets you compare prices and delivery times.

Tipping for Delivery in Hungary

Tipping Culture

Tipping is common in Hungary and Hungarians do generally tip service workers. For delivery, a tip of 10-15% is normal, or 200-500 HUF (roughly 0.50-1.30 EUR) per delivery. This is more expected than in some other Central European countries.

You can tip through the Wolt app after delivery or hand cash to the driver. If paying by card through the app, the in-app tip option is the most convenient. Drivers appreciate tips, especially for deliveries in bad weather, at late hours, or to difficult-to-find addresses.

Hotel & Airbnb Delivery

Getting Food to Your Door

Hotels in Budapest are generally delivery-friendly. Use the full street address and the hotel name in your delivery notes. Meeting the driver in the lobby is standard practice.

For Airbnbs, share the buzzer code or entry information with the driver through the in-app chat. Budapest apartment buildings typically have intercom systems. Make sure your host provides the buzzer code and name. Include the floor number and apartment number or name in the delivery notes.

Budapest is split between Buda and Pest by the Danube. Addresses on the Buda (hilly) side can be trickier for delivery. On the Pest side, the grid-like street layout makes deliveries more straightforward. In either case, being responsive to driver calls and messages helps ensure smooth delivery.

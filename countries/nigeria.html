English is the official language, so there are no language barriers. Foreign card acceptance can be tricky due to Nigerian banking regulations, so having cash on delivery as a backup is important. Tourist Reality Check

Nigeria is a growing but developing delivery market. Here is what tourists need to know.

Foreign Card Issues

Nigerian banking regulations can make foreign card acceptance tricky. Some apps may decline foreign cards or have intermittent issues with international payments. Glovo is the most reliable for foreign cards. Always have Nigerian naira in cash as a backup for cash on delivery. Exchange money at the airport, your hotel, or a bureau de change. Having both payment options available ensures you can always order.

Lagos Traffic Is Legendary

Lagos is famous for its traffic congestion, locally known as "go-slow." Delivery times can be highly unpredictable, especially during rush hours. A delivery that might take 20 minutes off-peak could take over an hour during peak traffic. Motorcycle delivery helps navigate congestion but times still vary. Order early for meals and avoid peak hours (7-10 AM, 4-8 PM) when possible.

Nigerian Food Is Incredible

Nigerian cuisine is one of West Africa's greatest food traditions. Jollof rice (the legendary dish), suya (spiced grilled meat), pounded yam with egusi or ogbono soup, pepper soup, moi moi (bean pudding), and akara (bean fritters) all deliver well. The food is flavorful, generous in portion, and affordable. Do not miss the opportunity to try authentic Nigerian dishes through delivery. Many restaurants specialize in specific regional cuisines.

Tech Scene Driving Improvement

Nigeria has one of Africa's most vibrant tech startup ecosystems (nicknamed "Silicon Lagoon" in Lagos). This means food delivery technology is improving rapidly. New apps launch regularly, existing apps get better, and the overall delivery experience is on an upward trajectory. What you read about Nigerian delivery today may be significantly better by the time you visit.

Tipping & Delivery Etiquette

Tipping in Nigeria is appreciated and valued by delivery workers.

Tips Appreciated (200-500 NGN)

Tipping delivery drivers 200-500 NGN (approximately $0.25-0.65 USD) is appreciated. Tips are not always expected but are valued, especially for deliveries in difficult traffic conditions or bad weather. When paying cash on delivery, rounding up is a common way to tip. Nigerians are warm and appreciative of generosity.

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Hotel delivery works in Lagos and Abuja's main areas.

Lagos Island and Victoria Island Well-Served

Hotels in Lagos Island, Victoria Island, Ikoyi, and Lekki areas have good delivery coverage. These are the main tourist and business areas with numerous hotel options. International hotel chains and local boutique hotels all accommodate food delivery. Drivers typically come to the hotel entrance or lobby. In Abuja, the Wuse and Maitama areas are well-served.

Gated Estates Have Security Protocols

Many residential areas and some hotels in Lagos and Abuja are in gated estates with security at the entrance. Delivery drivers are familiar with checking in at security gates. Provide the estate name, any access codes, and your specific building or unit number in delivery instructions. Security guards will verify and allow the driver through.

Top Food Delivery Apps in the UAE

The UAE has one of the most developed food delivery ecosystems in the world. Dubai and Abu Dhabi are global hubs with massive expat populations, meaning delivery apps cater heavily to international users. English is spoken everywhere, foreign cards are accepted across the board, and delivery is fast and reliable. Here is every app you need to know.

Talabat

Food Delivery — Dominant Platform
Top Pick

Talabat is the #1 food delivery app in the UAE and across the Middle East. Owned by Delivery Hero, it has the widest restaurant selection, covering everything from local shawarma joints to high-end restaurants. The app works flawlessly in English, accepts all major foreign credit cards, and delivers to virtually every address in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, and other emirates. Delivery times are impressively fast, often under 30 minutes. Talabat also offers grocery delivery through Talabat Mart, making it a one-stop solution for tourists.

Payment Foreign credit cards accepted (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
Coverage Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, all emirates
Language Full English support
Tourist-Friendly Excellent — works seamlessly for international users
Payability Score 88 / 100

Deliveroo

Food Delivery — Premium Option
Top Pick

Deliveroo is the premium food delivery option in the UAE. Originally from London, it has built a strong presence in Dubai and Abu Dhabi, with a curated selection of high-quality restaurants. Deliveroo is particularly popular in expat communities and upscale neighborhoods. It has the highest Payability Score of any app in the UAE at 90/100. The interface is polished, English is the default, and foreign cards are fully supported. If you are staying in a premium hotel or resort area, Deliveroo often has the best nearby restaurant options.

Payment Foreign credit cards accepted (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
Coverage Dubai, Abu Dhabi — strong in premium areas
Language Full English support (default language)
Tourist-Friendly Excellent — designed for international users
Payability Score 90 / 100

Careem

Super App — Rides, Food & More

Careem is the Middle East's homegrown super app, now owned by Uber. It started as a ride-hailing service and expanded into food delivery, grocery, and more. Careem's food delivery section has a solid restaurant selection, though not as extensive as Talabat. The advantage of Careem is that you may already have it installed for rides. It accepts foreign credit cards and works in English. Since Uber Eats does not operate in the UAE, Careem's food delivery effectively fills that niche for tourists who are familiar with the Uber ecosystem.

Payment Foreign credit cards accepted
Coverage Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah — major areas
Language Full English support
Tourist-Friendly Good — also useful for rides and payments
Payability Score 82 / 100

Noon Food

Food Delivery — Local Platform
Local Favorite

Noon Food is part of noon.com, the UAE's largest homegrown e-commerce platform. It entered the food delivery market to compete with Talabat and has been growing steadily. Noon Food often features aggressive promotions and discounts, making it a budget-friendly option. The app works in English and accepts card payments. While not as polished as Deliveroo or as comprehensive as Talabat, it can offer good deals and has decent coverage in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Payment Card payments accepted
Coverage Dubai, Abu Dhabi — expanding
Language English support
Tourist-Friendly Good — frequent promotions for new users
Payability Score 75 / 100

Grocery & Convenience Delivery

The UAE has an outstanding grocery delivery ecosystem. Whether you need to stock your hotel room or get essentials delivered, these services are fast, reliable, and tourist-friendly.

Carrefour (via Talabat & MAF App)

Grocery Delivery — Full Supermarket

Carrefour is the largest supermarket chain in the UAE and offers grocery delivery through multiple channels. You can order through Talabat Mart, the Carrefour app, or the MAF (Majid Al Futtaim) app. Full range of groceries, international products, fresh produce, and household items. Delivery is typically same-day or within a few hours.

InstaShop

Quick Commerce — Neighborhood Delivery

InstaShop connects you with local supermarkets, pharmacies, and specialty stores for quick delivery. Think of it as a marketplace for neighborhood shops. Great for getting specific items quickly, including international products, snacks, and health items. Coverage is excellent in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Noon Daily & Amazon.ae Fresh

Grocery Delivery — E-commerce Giants

Noon Daily offers grocery delivery through the noon.com ecosystem with competitive prices and fast delivery. Amazon.ae (formerly Souq.com) offers Amazon Fresh for grocery delivery in Dubai and Abu Dhabi. Both platforms accept foreign credit cards and provide English interfaces. Kibsons is also popular for fresh produce and organic products delivered to your door.

Tourist Reality Check

Here is what you need to know about ordering food delivery in the UAE as a tourist. Spoiler: it is incredibly easy.

The UAE Is a Delivery Paradise

The UAE is EXTREMELY tourist-friendly for food delivery. Dubai and Abu Dhabi have world-class delivery infrastructure built for a population that is over 85% expatriate. All major apps have English as a primary language. All accept foreign credit cards. Delivery is fast and reliable, with most orders arriving in 20-40 minutes. You will likely use delivery more here than almost any other country.

Summer Heat Makes Delivery Essential

From June through September, temperatures in the UAE can exceed 45°C (113°F). Walking to restaurants is simply not practical during peak summer hours. Food delivery becomes essential, not just convenient. The good news is that delivery drivers are equipped and trained for the heat, and food arrives well-packaged and insulated. Plan to rely on delivery apps heavily during summer visits.

Ramadan Delivery Is Fine for Tourists

During Ramadan, non-fasting tourists can still order food delivery. Many restaurants operate for delivery while closing their dine-in sections during fasting hours. Delivery is handled discreetly. Some restaurants may have reduced hours or limited menus, and delivery times may be slightly longer during iftar (breaking of fast) rush. But overall, delivery continues to work well throughout Ramadan.

Late-Night Delivery Is Outstanding

The UAE has a vibrant late-night culture. Many restaurants deliver until 2 AM or later, and some 24-hour options are available. Dubai in particular is a city that never sleeps, and the delivery apps reflect this. Whether you arrive on a late flight or want a midnight snack after desert safari, you will find plenty of delivery options.

Tipping & Delivery Etiquette

Tipping culture in the UAE is evolving. Here is what you should know about tipping delivery drivers.

Tipping Is Not Traditional but Appreciated

Tipping is not a traditional part of UAE culture, but it is becoming increasingly common, especially in Dubai. A tip of 5-10 AED (approximately $1.50-$3 USD) is appreciated by delivery drivers. Cash tips are particularly valued since drivers can keep them directly. Most apps offer in-app tipping as well. Delivery drivers in the UAE work long hours in extreme heat, so tips are a kind gesture even if not expected.

Contactless Delivery Available

All major UAE delivery apps offer contactless delivery. The driver will leave your order at the door or at a designated pickup point. This is standard practice and works well for hotel stays where you can specify "leave at reception" or "lobby pickup" in your delivery instructions.

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

The UAE has an excellent hotel delivery culture. Getting food delivered to your accommodation is standard practice.

Hotels Welcome Delivery

Most hotels in the UAE have dedicated delivery receiving areas. From budget hotels in Deira to luxury resorts on Palm Jumeirah, food delivery to hotels is completely normal and expected. Many five-star hotels have specific protocols for receiving delivery drivers at service entrances. Simply provide your hotel name, room number, and any special instructions.

Resort Delivery Works Too

Beach resorts, desert resorts, and even island hotels (like those on Yas Island or Saadiyat Island) accept food delivery. Drivers are familiar with resort layouts. For more remote resorts, delivery may take longer but is still possible. Check with your resort's front desk for their preferred delivery protocol.

Apartment Hotels & Serviced Apartments

If you are staying in a serviced apartment or apart-hotel (very common in Dubai), delivery is straightforward. Most buildings have intercoms and reception desks that handle deliveries. Use the building name and apartment number in your delivery instructions. Many buildings in Dubai Marina, JBR, and Downtown have dedicated delivery pickup areas.

Tourist Reality Check

Here is what it is actually like to use food delivery apps in Mexico as a tourist. No sugarcoating.

✅

Uber Eats is king for tourists in Mexico. If you already have the Uber app, you are set. It accepts your foreign credit card, has full English support, and has massive restaurant coverage in Mexico City, Cancun, Guadalajara, Playa del Carmen, Monterrey, and more. It is the easiest entry point for any visitor.

💡

Rappi is a game-changer super-app. Rappi does everything in Mexico ‚Äî restaurant food, grocery delivery, pharmacy runs, convenience store items, and even cash withdrawals from ATMs delivered to your door. It accepts foreign cards and is hugely popular across Latin America. If you are traveling through multiple LATAM countries, download Rappi once and use it everywhere.

💰

DiDi Food has the best promotions. The Chinese-backed delivery platform often runs aggressive discounts and coupon campaigns to win market share. If you want to save money, check DiDi Food for deals. It accepts foreign cards and covers CDMX, Guadalajara, Monterrey, and growing into more cities.

🚲

Coverage is excellent in tourist areas. CDMX (Mexico City), Cancun, Playa del Carmen, Guadalajara, Puerto Vallarta, and Monterrey all have robust delivery infrastructure. Smaller towns and rural areas will have limited or no coverage. Beach resort areas are well-served.

🍴

Cash on delivery is available and popular. If you prefer paying in Mexican pesos, all three major apps (Uber Eats, Rappi, DiDi Food) support cash payment. This can be useful if you want to avoid foreign transaction fees on your card. Have small bills ready.

🌎

Spanish helps but is not essential. Uber Eats has strong English support. Rappi and DiDi Food are primarily in Spanish, but the interfaces are intuitive enough to navigate. Restaurant menus are mostly in Spanish, so knowing basic food terms (pollo = chicken, cerdo = pork, res = beef) is handy.

Tipping Delivery Drivers

Tipping is important in Mexican culture and delivery drivers depend on tips. Here is what to know:

  • Typical tip: 10-15% of your order total, or 20-30 MXN minimum (about $1-2 USD)
  • Tipping is expected ‚Äî Mexican delivery drivers rely on propinas (tips) to earn a living wage
  • All major apps (Uber Eats, Rappi, DiDi Food) have in-app tipping after delivery
  • Cash tips at the door are also common and appreciated
  • During rain, late-night orders, or holiday periods, consider tipping more generously
  • A 20 MXN tip is considered acceptable; 30-50 MXN is generous

Hotel & Accommodation Delivery

Getting food delivered to your accommodation in Mexico is generally straightforward, but there are some things to know about different property types.

🏖

All-inclusive resorts may not allow outside delivery. Many all-inclusive resorts in Cancun and the Riviera Maya restrict outside food deliveries as part of their policies. Check with your resort before ordering. Some have designated pickup areas at the gate.

🏨

City hotels are delivery-friendly. Hotels in Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey, and other urban areas are very used to delivery drivers arriving. Drivers typically meet you in the lobby. Leave clear instructions in the app.

🏠

Airbnbs and apartments work great. If you are staying in an Airbnb or vacation rental, food delivery is seamless. Drop a pin for your exact location, include any gate codes or building access info in the delivery notes, and you are good to go.

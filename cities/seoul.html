Baemin (Baedal Minjok, meaning "Delivery Nation") is South Korea's undisputed food delivery king. Nearly every restaurant in Seoul is on Baemin, from hole-in-the-wall chimaek (chicken and beer) joints to fine dining. The app handles millions of orders daily and is deeply woven into Korean daily life. Best for Late Night Delivery

Seoul is arguably the best city in the world for late-night delivery. The culture of "ya-sik" (late-night food) runs deep.

Baemin 24/7

Baemin is essentially a 24-hour service in Seoul. Korean fried chicken joints, jjajangmyeon (black bean noodle) restaurants, and tteokbokki shops keep delivering until 3-4 AM. The famous "chicken and beer" (chimaek) culture means fried chicken delivery is available at virtually any hour. This is one of Seoul's greatest pleasures — ordering a box of crispy Korean fried chicken at 2 AM is a rite of passage.

Convenience Stores as Backup

If you can't navigate the Korean delivery apps at night, Seoul's ubiquitous convenience stores (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven, Emart24) are open 24/7 and have surprisingly great food. Hot meals, kimbap, ramen, fried chicken — all available within a 5-minute walk of almost any location in Seoul. These are a tourist's secret weapon when delivery apps prove too challenging.

Best for Cheap Delivery

Seoul delivery is already affordable, and the competition between apps keeps prices low.

Baemin for Budget Eats

Baemin has the lowest delivery fees in Seoul, with many restaurants offering free delivery on orders above 15,000-20,000 won ($11-15). The "Baemin 1" single-rider service costs more but "Baemin" (regular) delivery is very affordable. For tourists who can access it, a full Korean meal delivered rarely exceeds 10,000-15,000 won ($7-11) including delivery.

Group Orders Save Money

Seoul delivery culture is built around group orders. Many restaurants have minimum order amounts (12,000-18,000 won). Ordering with friends splits the delivery fee and meets minimums easily. Solo travelers: look for restaurants with no or low minimum orders on Coupang Eats, which specializes in single-rider delivery with no minimum.

Best Grocery Delivery

Seoul's grocery delivery is world-class — Coupang Rocket Fresh literally delivers overnight.

Coupang Rocket Fresh

Grocery — Dawn Delivery
Best Option

Coupang Rocket Fresh is Korea's premier grocery delivery service. Order before midnight and your groceries arrive by 7 AM the next morning ("dawn delivery"). The selection includes fresh produce, Korean snacks, drinks, household items, and imported goods. The service requires a Coupang account (Korean phone helpful), but if you can set it up, it's an incredible way to stock your accommodation with Korean treats, instant noodles, fruit, and breakfast items.

Neighborhood Coverage

Seoul has excellent delivery coverage everywhere, but some neighborhoods are more tourist-accessible than others.

Gangnam — Excellent

Gangnam has outstanding delivery coverage with every app. High-end Korean restaurants, trendy cafes, and international cuisine are all available. Delivery times are fast (15-25 minutes). The area's density of restaurants means massive selection on Baemin and Coupang Eats.

Hongdae / Mapo — Excellent

The university district has phenomenal delivery options. Cheap Korean food, trendy restaurants, and late-night spots are abundant. Great for budget-conscious travelers. Baemin coverage is dense here with many student-friendly prices.

Myeongdong — Good

The major shopping district has good delivery coverage. Tourist hotels here receive deliveries regularly. Coverage is strong but Myeongdong is more of a "go out and eat" area, so delivery is less commonly used by locals here.

Itaewon — Good (Most English-Friendly)

Itaewon is the international district and your best bet for English-friendly delivery experiences. Restaurants here are more likely to have English menus on delivery apps. Shuttle delivery service has its best coverage here. If you're determined to use delivery as a tourist, stay in or near Itaewon for the easiest experience.

Jongno — Good

The historic district around Gyeongbokgung has good delivery coverage. Traditional Korean restaurants and modern eateries coexist. Hotels in this area receive deliveries well. The narrow alleys of Bukchon Hanok Village can be tricky for delivery navigation.

Tourist Usability Checklist

Seoul is the most challenging major city for tourist food delivery. Here is the honest reality.

Foreign Credit Cards

Coupang Eats: Sometimes works with foreign Visa/Mastercard — try it but no guarantee. Baemin: No. Yogiyo: No. Shuttle: Some foreign cards. Korea's payment ecosystem is uniquely restrictive for foreigners. Prepaid Tmoney cards (available at convenience stores) don't work for delivery. Your best backup: cash payment (available on some apps) or asking hotel staff for help.

Language Barrier Is Real

Coupang Eats: Korean (slightly navigable with photos). Baemin: Korean only. Yogiyo: Korean only. Shuttle: English. Use Google Translate's camera to read menus. Most restaurant names and food descriptions are entirely in Korean. Photos help, but expect some guesswork. Restaurant reviews and ratings can guide you even without reading Korean.

Korean Phone Number

A Korean phone number is very helpful for all delivery apps. SMS verification is required for registration. You can get a Korean SIM at Incheon Airport (KT, SK Telecom, LG U+) or from convenience stores. An eSIM from Airalo also works. Without a Korean number, your options are severely limited. This is the single most important thing to arrange for Seoul delivery access.

Hotel Delivery

Hotels work well for delivery in Seoul. Drivers will come to the lobby. Guesthouses and Airbnbs may need more specific instructions. Jjimjilbangs (Korean spas/saunas) generally don't accept food delivery. Convenience stores remain the ultimate backup — CU, GS25, and 7-Eleven are on every block with excellent prepared food options.

Delivery Etiquette in Seoul

Seoul's delivery culture is hyper-efficient. Here is what to know.

Tipping Not Expected

Like Japan, South Korea does not have a tipping culture. Delivery riders do not expect tips and there's typically no tipping option in the apps. Just be polite and prompt in receiving your food.

Drivers Will Call

Seoul delivery drivers frequently call when arriving. This is a challenge if you don't speak Korean. Have Google Translate ready, or add very detailed delivery notes. For apartments and officetel buildings, provide the access code and floor number. Many drivers leave food at the door ("door delivery"), which helps avoid language barriers.

Speed Is King

Seoul delivery is blazingly fast — 20-30 minutes is typical, and many Coupang Eats orders arrive in 15-20 minutes. Korean riders are some of the fastest in the world, navigating Seoul's dense urban landscape on scooters. Don't be surprised when food arrives faster than the app estimate.

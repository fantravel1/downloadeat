Best for Late Night Delivery

Tokyo never truly sleeps, and neither does its food delivery scene — at least in the right neighborhoods.

Uber Eats Late Night in Tokyo

Uber Eats is your best bet for late-night delivery in Tokyo. In entertainment districts like Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Roppongi, many restaurants stay open on the platform until 1-2 AM or even later on Friday and Saturday nights. You'll find ramen, gyudon (beef bowl), karaage (fried chicken), and fast food chains available well past midnight. Outside central areas, options drop off significantly after 10-11 PM.

OniGO for Midnight Snacks

For non-restaurant items at odd hours, OniGO delivers convenience store essentials (onigiri, snacks, drinks, instant noodles) in about 10 minutes in central Tokyo. It's like having a konbini come to you without stepping outside. Great for that 2 AM craving when you don't want to get dressed.

Best for Cheap Delivery

Tokyo's delivery fees can add up. Here is how to keep costs down.

Demae-can Has the Lowest Fees

Demae-can generally offers the lowest delivery fees in Tokyo, frequently running promotions with free delivery on orders above a certain amount. Many local restaurants price their food lower on Demae-can compared to Uber Eats. The catch: you need Japanese language skills and local payment to use it. For tourists, Uber Eats Eats Pass (subscription) can reduce fees if you're ordering frequently during a longer stay.

Tourist Budget Tips

On Uber Eats Tokyo, delivery fees typically range from 50-350 yen. Order from nearby restaurants to minimize fees. Look for "Free Delivery" promotions in the app. During off-peak hours (2-5 PM), fees tend to be lower. Also check for first-time user promo codes — Uber Eats Japan frequently offers discounts of 1,000-2,000 yen for new users.

Neighborhood Coverage

Delivery coverage varies by neighborhood in Tokyo. Here is what to expect in the most popular areas.

Shibuya / Shinjuku — Excellent

The best coverage in Tokyo. Hundreds of restaurants on every app. Uber Eats, Demae-can, Wolt, and menu all have dense coverage. Late-night options are abundant. Delivery times are fast (15-25 minutes typical). This is ground zero for Tokyo's food delivery scene.

Ginza / Marunouchi — Excellent

Premium dining options with excellent delivery coverage. You'll find high-end sushi, tempura, and international cuisine. Uber Eats has strong coverage here. Business hotel delivery is seamless. Expect slightly higher menu prices reflecting the upscale neighborhood.

Asakusa — Good

The traditional tourist district has good but not outstanding delivery coverage. Uber Eats works well here. You'll find a nice mix of traditional Japanese food and modern options. Some smaller streets near Senso-ji can be tricky for drivers to navigate, so use precise GPS pins.

Roppongi — Excellent

International dining hub with excellent delivery options. Great late-night coverage given the nightlife district. Many international restaurants available. Uber Eats has some of its best Tokyo coverage here, with deliveries available into the early morning hours on weekends.

Ikebukuro — Good

Solid delivery coverage with a mix of Japanese and international options. Uber Eats and Demae-can both work well. Less late-night availability than Shibuya or Roppongi, but still plenty of options until 11 PM-midnight.

Suburbs (Tama, Edogawa, etc.) — Varies

Outer suburbs have much more limited delivery coverage. Uber Eats reaches some suburban areas, but restaurant selection drops significantly. Demae-can often has better suburban coverage due to its larger network. Delivery times are longer (30-45+ minutes). If staying in suburban Tokyo, set expectations accordingly.

Tourist Usability Checklist

Here is a quick reference for what works and what doesn't when ordering food delivery as a tourist in Tokyo.

Foreign Credit Cards

Uber Eats: Yes — Visa, Mastercard, Amex all work. Wolt: Yes. Foodpanda: Partial. Demae-can: No — Japanese cards only. menu: No. For tourists, Uber Eats is the only fully reliable option for foreign card payments in Tokyo.

English Language Support

Uber Eats: Full English UI and restaurant menus. Wolt: Good English. Foodpanda: Partial. Demae-can: Japanese only. menu: Japanese only. Restaurant names and descriptions are often in Japanese even on English apps, but photos make ordering easy.

Local Phone Number

Not needed for Uber Eats — your international number works fine. Other apps benefit from or require a Japanese number for SMS verification. If you plan to use multiple apps, a Japanese eSIM (Airalo, Ubigi) or airport SIM is recommended but not essential for Uber Eats.

Hotel Delivery

Works great in Tokyo. Major hotel chains (APA, Toyoko Inn, Hilton, Marriott) all accommodate delivery drivers in the lobby. Add your hotel name in the delivery notes. Capsule hotels: meet the driver at the entrance. Traditional ryokans: check first, some don't allow outside food. Airbnbs work well — just provide the exact address and apartment access instructions.

Delivery Etiquette in Tokyo

Tokyo has its own customs around food delivery. Follow these guidelines to be a respectful customer.

No Tipping — Seriously

Japan has no tipping culture, and tipping can actually cause confusion. Delivery drivers do not expect tips. While Uber Eats has an optional tipping feature, Japanese drivers are not accustomed to receiving tips. Simply be polite, say "arigatou gozaimasu" (thank you), and you are being the perfect customer.

Contactless Delivery Is Standard

Since the pandemic, contactless delivery ("okihai" / ÁΩÆ„ÅçÈÖç) has become the norm in Tokyo. The driver leaves food at your door and sends a photo. This eliminates language barriers entirely and is the most common delivery method. Select it in your Uber Eats settings for a hassle-free experience.

Use a GPS Pin, Not Just an Address

Japanese addresses are notoriously confusing — blocks are numbered, not streets. Always drop a pin on the map in the delivery app rather than typing an address. This prevents confusion for both you and the driver. For hotels, using the hotel name in addition to the pin ensures the driver finds the right entrance.

Drivers Rarely Call

Unlike many countries, Japanese delivery drivers almost never call customers. They navigate via GPS and communicate through the app if needed. This is great news for tourists since you won't face phone language barriers. Just make sure your delivery location pin is accurate.

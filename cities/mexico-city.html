Best for Late Night Delivery

Mexico City has a vibrant late-night food culture, and delivery apps capitalize on it.

Uber Eats Late Night in CDMX

Uber Eats has the best late-night delivery in Mexico City. In Roma, Condesa, Polanco, and Centro, many taquerias and fast food joints stay on the platform until midnight or later. The famous late-night taco culture means you can get tacos al pastor, suadero, and birria delivered past midnight. On weekends, options extend even later in nightlife-heavy areas.

Rappi After Hours

Rappi also has strong late-night coverage, including its "Rappi Turbo" quick delivery stores that stay open late. You can order snacks, drinks, and quick meals from Rappi's dark stores when regular restaurants have closed. Plus, Rappi's "Favor" service lets you request items from any 24-hour OXXO convenience store near you.

Best for Cheap Delivery

Mexico City delivery is already very affordable. Here is how to stretch your pesos further.

DiDi Food for the Best Deals

DiDi Food consistently has the lowest delivery fees in CDMX, often 50-70% cheaper than Uber Eats for the same restaurant. They run aggressive promotions for new users and frequent 2x1 deals. A delivered meal on DiDi Food can cost as little as 80-120 MXN ($4.50-7) including delivery and tip. Check DiDi Food first if price is your priority.

Budget Tourist Tips

On Uber Eats, look for "2x1" (two-for-one) promotions. Order from fondas (home-style Mexican eateries) for complete meals under 100 MXN ($5.50). Rappi frequently offers free delivery promotions. All three apps offer new-user discount codes — create accounts on all of them to maximize first-order savings. Mexico City is incredibly affordable for delivery compared to most global cities.

Neighborhood Coverage

CDMX is enormous. Delivery coverage varies by neighborhood, though tourist areas are well-served.

Roma / Condesa — Excellent

The absolute best delivery coverage in CDMX. These trendy, walkable neighborhoods are packed with restaurants, cafes, and food spots all on delivery apps. You'll find traditional Mexican food, international cuisine, vegan options, and hipster cafes. Late-night tacos are especially strong here. This is digital nomad central, and the delivery infrastructure matches.

Polanco — Excellent

The upscale district has excellent coverage with premium restaurant options. Fine dining, sushi, steakhouses, and high-end Mexican cuisine. Delivery is fast and reliable. Hotels in Polanco (including many international chains) receive deliveries routinely.

Centro Histórico — Good

The historic center has good delivery coverage with many traditional Mexican restaurants. Navigation can be tricky on pedestrian-only streets near the Zócalo. Drivers on motorcycles handle the tight streets well. Good selection of fondas and traditional eateries.

Coyoacán — Good

The charming neighborhood near Frida Kahlo's museum has good delivery coverage. Great for traditional Mexican food and local markets. Delivery times may be slightly longer due to the distance from central CDMX, but all major apps operate here with solid restaurant selection.

Santa Fe — Good

The business district has good delivery coverage, especially during lunch hours. More corporate dining options. Coverage from all major apps. Weekends have fewer options as many restaurants cater to the office crowd.

Tourist Usability Checklist

Mexico City is one of the most tourist-friendly cities for food delivery. Almost everything just works.

Foreign Credit Cards

Uber Eats: Yes — all major cards. Rappi: Yes. DiDi Food: Yes, generally. Cornershop: Yes. Mexico City is a dream for tourist delivery — all major apps accept foreign credit cards without issues. This is one of the best cities in the world for foreign card compatibility.

English Language Support

Uber Eats: English UI available. Rappi: Primarily Spanish, navigable. DiDi Food: Primarily Spanish. Cornershop: Some English. Restaurant menus are in Spanish, but food photos make ordering easy. In Roma and Polanco, some restaurants have English menu descriptions.

Local Phone Number

Not required for any major app. Your international phone number works fine with Uber Eats, Rappi, and DiDi Food. A Mexican SIM is cheap but unnecessary for delivery purposes.

Hotel Delivery

Works great everywhere in CDMX. Hotels, Airbnbs, hostels — all work for delivery. For apartments with a portero (doorman), the driver will leave food at the front. Hotels: drivers come to the lobby. The large expat and digital nomad community means delivery infrastructure is extremely well-developed for tourist accommodations.

Delivery Etiquette in Mexico City

CDMX delivery culture is friendly and tip-oriented. Here is what to know.

Always Tip Your Delivery Driver

Tipping is expected and important in Mexico City. Delivery riders work long hours in heavy traffic and pollution, often on bicycles or motorcycles. The standard tip is 10-15% of the order total, or at minimum 20-30 MXN ($1-1.70). Both Uber Eats and Rappi have in-app tipping. Please tip generously — it makes a real difference for drivers' income.

Drivers May Call or Message

CDMX drivers often call when they can't find the entrance or need buzzer codes. Basic Spanish helps: "primer piso" (first floor), "puerta roja" (red door). Most drivers are patient and friendly. Add detailed delivery notes in advance to minimize calls. For Airbnbs, always include the gate or buzzer code.

Rainy Season Affects Delivery

During CDMX's rainy season (June-October), afternoon thunderstorms can cause delivery delays and surcharges. Delivery fees spike during heavy rain as fewer riders are available. Order earlier in the day or be prepared for longer waits during rainstorms. Drivers are still dedicated — they just move slower in the rain.

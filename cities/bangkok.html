All Available Apps in Bangkok

Bangkok has one of the most competitive food delivery markets in the world. Here is every major app, ranked by tourist-friendliness.

LINE MAN

Food Delivery — Local Favorite
Local Favorite

LINE MAN is the Thai delivery powerhouse, deeply integrated with LINE (Thailand's dominant messaging app). It has the largest restaurant selection in Bangkok, including countless street food stalls and small local eateries that aren't on Grab. Delivery fees are generally lower, and promotions are more frequent. The catch for tourists: you'll need a Thai SIM card for LINE registration, the interface is partially in Thai, and foreign card support is limited. If you get a Thai SIM, LINE MAN unlocks a whole new level of Bangkok food delivery.

Payment Thai payment preferred — limited foreign card support
Coverage Widest restaurant selection in Bangkok
Language Mixed Thai/English — partially navigable
Tourist-Friendly Thai SIM needed for LINE registration
Payability Score 45 / 100

Foodpanda

Food Delivery — International Platform

Foodpanda has been a major player in Bangkok for years and maintains strong restaurant partnerships across the city. The interface is clean and English-friendly, and it accepts some foreign cards. Coverage is good in central Bangkok, though it has lost some market share to Grab and LINE MAN. If you already have a Foodpanda account from another Asian country, it may work in Bangkok. Useful as a secondary option with different restaurant deals.

Payment Some foreign cards accepted
Coverage Good in central Bangkok
Language Good English support
Tourist-Friendly Good — familiar interface for travelers
Payability Score 72 / 100

Robinhood

Food Delivery — Zero Commission Thai Platform

Robinhood is a Thai-owned delivery platform backed by SCB (Siam Commercial Bank). Its unique selling point: zero commission charges to restaurants, meaning menu prices are often lower than on Grab or LINE MAN. The app is growing fast in Bangkok. However, it's primarily designed for the Thai market with limited English and requires Thai payment methods or cash on delivery. A good option if you speak Thai or want to support a platform that treats restaurants fairly.

Payment Thai payment or cash on delivery
Coverage Good in central Bangkok, growing
Language Primarily Thai
Tourist-Friendly Difficult for non-Thai speakers
Payability Score 30 / 100

ShopeeFood

Food Delivery — E-commerce Crossover

ShopeeFood is the food delivery arm of Shopee, Southeast Asia's massive e-commerce platform. It offers aggressive promotions and discounts to gain market share. If you already use Shopee for online shopping, the food delivery feature is built into the same app. Coverage in Bangkok is growing but not yet matching Grab or LINE MAN. Primarily aimed at the local market with Thai-language dominance.

Best for Late Night Delivery

Bangkok is a city that barely sleeps, and its delivery scene reflects that energy.

Grab After Midnight

Grab is your best bet for late-night delivery in Bangkok. Along Sukhumvit, Silom, and Khao San Road, many restaurants stay on the platform until 1-2 AM. You'll find pad thai, som tam, grilled meats, and fast food available well past midnight. Weekend nights have even more options as Bangkok's famous nightlife keeps kitchens open later.

Street Food Never Sleeps

Bangkok's legendary street food scene extends to delivery apps. Many street stalls listed on Grab and LINE MAN operate until very late. Night markets and street food vendors in areas like Yaowarat (Chinatown) and Ratchada keep serving and delivering into the small hours. Don't expect fancy presentation, but do expect incredible flavor.

Best for Cheap Delivery

Bangkok is already one of the cheapest cities for food delivery in the world. Here is how to save even more.

LINE MAN for the Best Deals

LINE MAN consistently offers the lowest delivery fees in Bangkok, often 10-25 baht ($0.30-0.70) for nearby restaurants. They run aggressive promotions, especially for new users. The catch: you need a Thai SIM for LINE registration and payment options for tourists are limited. If you can set it up, your Bangkok food budget will stretch much further.

Budget Tips for Tourists on Grab

On Grab, delivery fees in Bangkok are already low (typically 15-49 baht / $0.40-1.40). Order from nearby restaurants to minimize fees. Check the "Promotions" tab for discount codes — Grab frequently offers buy-one-get-one deals and free delivery promos. A full meal including delivery rarely exceeds 150-200 baht ($4-6) for local Thai food. That's cheaper than most sit-down meals at tourist restaurants.

Best Grocery Delivery

Stock up your hotel or serviced apartment with Bangkok's convenient grocery delivery options.

Tops (via GrabMart)

Grocery Delivery — Full Supermarket
Best for Tourists

Tops is one of Thailand's major supermarket chains, and you can order from them directly through GrabMart (inside the Grab app). This means you can use your foreign credit card for a full grocery delivery. Selection includes fresh produce, Thai snacks, drinks, toiletries, and imported goods. Delivery typically takes 30-60 minutes. Perfect for stocking up on water, fruit, and breakfast items.

GrabMart

Convenience & Grocery — Quick Delivery

Beyond Tops, GrabMart partners with 7-Eleven, Family Mart, and various convenience stores in Bangkok for quick delivery of essentials. Need water, snacks, sunscreen, or a phone charger? GrabMart can get it to you in 20-30 minutes using your foreign card through the main Grab app. Incredibly convenient for hotel-bound evenings.

Neighborhood Coverage

Bangkok is a massive city. Here is how delivery coverage varies across the most popular areas for tourists and locals.

Sukhumvit — Excellent

The backbone of tourist Bangkok has the best delivery coverage in the city. From Nana to On Nut, every app operates here with massive restaurant selection. International cuisine, Thai street food, high-end dining — it's all available. Late-night options are especially strong along the lower Sukhumvit sois.

Silom / Sathorn — Excellent

The business district has excellent coverage with a wide mix of office lunch spots and evening dining. Grab and LINE MAN both have dense coverage here. Silom's famous street food (especially at night) translates well to delivery, and nearby Sathorn condos are well-served. Fast delivery times during all hours.

Khao San Road — Good

The famous backpacker area has good delivery coverage but with some quirks. The narrow lanes and heavy foot traffic can make finding a delivery drop-off point tricky. Grab works well — just set a clear meeting point. Lots of local Thai food and tourist-friendly restaurants available. Coverage drops slightly in the sois around Banglamphu.

Thonglor — Excellent

Bangkok's trendy neighborhood has phenomenal delivery options. This is where Bangkok's foodie scene thrives, and every hipster cafe and upscale Thai restaurant is on the delivery apps. Excellent coverage from all platforms with great late-night options from the area's many bars and late-night eateries.

Old Town (Rattanakosin) — Fair

The historic area around the Grand Palace and Wat Pho has fair but not outstanding delivery coverage. The narrow streets and heavy tourist foot traffic make deliveries slower. Grab works but with fewer restaurant options compared to Sukhumvit or Silom. For a wider selection, you may want to explore nearby Ari or Bangrak areas in the app.

Suburbs — Varies

Outer Bangkok areas like Bang Na, Nonthaburi, and Lat Phrao still have delivery coverage from Grab and LINE MAN, but restaurant selection is smaller and delivery times are longer. Most suburban areas are well-covered for basic needs, but you won't have the same variety as central Bangkok.

Tourist Usability Checklist

What works and what doesn't for tourists ordering delivery in Bangkok.

Foreign Credit Cards

Grab: Yes — Visa and Mastercard work reliably. Foodpanda: Yes, most cards accepted. LINE MAN: Limited — Thai payment preferred. Robinhood: No — Thai payment or cash. ShopeeFood: No. Grab is the only fully reliable option for foreign cards in Bangkok.

English Language Support

Grab: Full English. Foodpanda: Good English. LINE MAN: Partial — menus often in Thai with photos. Robinhood: Mostly Thai. ShopeeFood: Mostly Thai. Restaurant names and descriptions are often in Thai across all apps, but photos make ordering manageable.

Thai SIM Card

A Thai SIM is very helpful for LINE MAN (which requires LINE, which requires a Thai number for best experience). Grab works fine with your international number. Thai SIMs are extremely cheap (100-300 baht / $3-9) and available at any 7-Eleven, airport counter, or AIS/DTAC/True shop. Getting one unlocks LINE MAN and makes your Bangkok delivery experience much richer.

Hotel & Condo Delivery

Hotels and condos work great in Bangkok for delivery. Hotels: drivers come to the lobby; add hotel name in notes. Condos: drivers leave food at the lobby security desk; tell security to expect a delivery. Serviced apartments work the same as condos. Hostels: meet the driver at the front entrance. Bangkok accommodation staff are very accustomed to food delivery.

Delivery Etiquette in Bangkok

Bangkok has a relaxed delivery culture, but a few customs are worth knowing.

Tipping Is Appreciated but Not Expected

Unlike Japan where tipping is unwelcome, Bangkok drivers appreciate small tips. On Grab, you can tip in-app after delivery. 20-40 baht ($0.60-1.10) is generous. It's not mandatory, but delivery riders in Bangkok work hard in intense heat and traffic. A small tip goes a long way.

Drivers May Call

Bangkok delivery drivers frequently call when they arrive, especially at large condo complexes. If you don't speak Thai, a simple "lobby" or "front" works. Many drivers use LINE chat to communicate. For a smooth experience, add detailed delivery notes in English ("meet at lobby," "blue building, gate 2") — Grab's in-app chat also has auto-translate.

Traffic Affects Everything

Bangkok's legendary traffic jams affect delivery times significantly. Rush hour (7-9 AM, 5-8 PM) can double delivery times. Rain makes it even worse. Order from nearby restaurants and avoid peak hours when possible. Motorcycle riders navigate traffic better than cars, and most Bangkok delivery is done by motorbike, which helps.

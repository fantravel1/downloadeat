Best for Late Night Delivery

Paris is not a late-night delivery powerhouse. Here is what to expect.

Uber Eats Until Midnight-1 AM

Uber Eats has the best late-night coverage in Paris, with many restaurants open until midnight in central arrondissements (1st-4th, 10th-11th). In nightlife areas like Bastille, Oberkampf, and parts of Le Marais, some options extend to 1 AM. Expect kebabs, pizza, burgers, and some Asian food. After 1 AM, options are very scarce — Paris truly sleeps earlier than cities like Tokyo or Bangkok.

Deliveroo Late Night

Deliveroo has similar late-night hours to Uber Eats. Selection is slightly smaller after 11 PM but quality remains good. Both apps show you estimated delivery times and available restaurants in real-time, so you can quickly compare what's open near your location.

Best for Cheap Delivery

Paris is expensive, but delivery apps can actually save you money compared to restaurant dining.

Just Eat for Budget Meals

Just Eat typically offers the lowest delivery fees and most budget-friendly restaurants in Paris. Kebab plates, pizza, Chinese food, and other everyday options can be delivered for 8-12 EUR including delivery. Many restaurants offer meal deals (formules) that are cheaper than ordering individual items.

Tourist Budget Tips

On Uber Eats, look for "Uber One" (subscription for free delivery) if staying a week or more. Order during off-peak hours for lower delivery fees. Many Parisian boulangeries (bakeries) now deliver sandwiches and quiches through apps — excellent quality at 5-8 EUR. Avoid peak lunch hours (12-1:30 PM) when fees surge.

Neighborhood Coverage

Paris's 20 arrondissements all have delivery coverage, with central areas having the best selection.

Le Marais (3rd-4th arr.) — Excellent

One of the best areas for delivery in Paris. Dense restaurant scene covering French, Jewish, Asian, and international cuisines. Excellent evening and late-night options. The historic streets can be tricky for drivers — include specific building details in delivery notes.

Saint-Germain (6th arr.) — Excellent

The Left Bank intellectual district has outstanding delivery coverage with a French bistro emphasis. Expect croque monsieurs, galettes, tartares, and excellent pastries. Deliveroo has particularly strong coverage here with several upscale exclusive partnerships.

Montmartre (18th arr.) — Good

The hilly tourist neighborhood has good coverage, though the steep streets and stairs can slow delivery. Uber Eats and Deliveroo both work well. Include precise location details since GPS can be unreliable on Montmartre's winding cobblestone streets.

Bastille (11th arr.) — Excellent

The vibrant nightlife district has excellent delivery coverage with diverse options. Great for late-night orders. Strong selection of both French and international restaurants. One of the best neighborhoods for delivery variety in Paris.

16th Arrondissement — Good

The upscale residential area has good but less dense coverage. Fewer restaurants per block compared to central Paris. Delivery times may be slightly longer. Quality options are available but expect a smaller selection than Le Marais or Bastille.

Banlieue (Suburbs) — Varies

Inner suburbs (Boulogne-Billancourt, Vincennes, Saint-Denis) have decent coverage from Uber Eats. Outer suburbs have increasingly limited options. If staying outside Paris proper, check app coverage before booking your accommodation.

Tourist Usability Checklist

Paris is very tourist-friendly for delivery. One unique challenge: the digicode.

Foreign Credit Cards

Uber Eats: Yes. Deliveroo: Yes. Just Eat: Yes. Frichti: Yes. All major Paris delivery apps accept foreign credit cards without issues. Paris is one of the easiest European cities for tourist food delivery payment.

English Language Support

Uber Eats: Full English. Deliveroo: Full English. Just Eat: Some English. Frichti: Primarily French. Restaurant menus may be in French, but food photos and common dish names make ordering manageable even without French.

Local Phone Number

Not required for any app. Your international phone number works fine with Uber Eats, Deliveroo, and Just Eat. No French SIM needed for delivery purposes.

Apartments Need the Digicode

Most Paris apartment buildings have a digicode (numeric keypad entry). Always include your digicode in delivery notes. Also include: floor number ("3ème étage"), apartment position ("gauche" for left, "droite" for right), staircase letter if applicable ("escalier B"), and whether there's an elevator. Without the digicode, drivers cannot enter the building and will call you. Hotels don't have this issue — just include the hotel name.

Delivery Etiquette in Paris

Delivery etiquette in Paris is relaxed but follows French customs.

Tipping Is Optional but Appreciated

Tipping delivery drivers in Paris is not mandatory (service is generally included in French pricing), but a small tip of 1-2 EUR is appreciated. Uber Eats and Deliveroo both have in-app tipping options. In rain or during late hours, a tip is a nice gesture.

Say "Bonjour" and "Merci"

Even in a quick delivery handoff, greeting the driver with "bonjour" and saying "merci" goes a long way in France. It's a small cultural gesture that Parisians appreciate. If you're meeting the driver at the door, a smile and a "merci, bonne soirée" (thank you, good evening) is perfect etiquette.

Sunday Closures

Many Parisian restaurants close on Sundays and Mondays. Delivery options may be more limited on these days. Plan ahead for Sunday meals — larger chains and international restaurants are more likely to be open. Stock up on groceries on Saturday if you want to cook on Sunday.
